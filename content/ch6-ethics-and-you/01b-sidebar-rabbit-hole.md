You search for "cool card tricks."

Ten minutes later: magic show highlights.

Then: "mind-blowing illusions EXPLAINED."

Then: "things they don't want you to know."

Then: conspiracy videos about aliens.

Wait -- what just happened?

Each recommendation made sense *on its own*. Card tricks are related to magic shows. Magic shows are related to illusions. Illusions are related to "mind-blowing" content. "Mind-blowing" content is related to... well, that's where it gets slippery.

This is the **rabbit hole effect**. Every single step feels like a tiny, reasonable jump. But the *path* leads somewhere totally unexpected -- and sometimes somewhere pretty weird.

It happens because the algorithm optimizes for **one video at a time**. It asks: "Given what you just watched, what's the best NEXT video?" It never steps back and asks: "Wait, where is this whole session heading? Is this person still enjoying themselves, or are they sliding into bizarre territory?"

Imagine walking through a city where every time you reach an intersection, someone says "Turn left -- there's something cool!" Each individual suggestion might be fine. But after 20 left turns, you're going in circles in a neighborhood you never meant to visit.

**Think about it:** Next time you notice your recommendations getting weird, scroll back and trace the path. You'll see the tiny steps that got you there. Recognizing the pattern is the first step to breaking it.
