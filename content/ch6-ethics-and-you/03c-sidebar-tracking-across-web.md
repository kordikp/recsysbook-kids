So far we've talked about what ONE app knows about you. But there's something darker going on.

**Some companies track you across MANY websites at once.** They do this using tiny invisible files called **third-party cookies** or hidden tracking pixels. Here's how it works:

You visit a news website. Hidden in the page is a tiny piece of code from a company you've never heard of — like Outbrain, Taboola, or dozens of ad networks. That code drops a cookie on your browser. Now when you visit a DIFFERENT website that uses the same tracking company, they recognize you. "Oh, this is the same person who read about skateboarding on that news site."

Over time, these companies build a profile of everywhere you go online — without you ever signing up for their service or even knowing they exist.

**This is NOT the same as YouTube recommending videos.** YouTube uses YOUR data on THEIR app to help YOU. Cross-site trackers use YOUR data across EVERYONE's apps to help ADVERTISERS find you. Big difference.

**What's being done about it?**

Honestly? Not enough yet. The EU is trying — they want to ban third-party cookies and restrict cross-site tracking. Google promised to block them in Chrome but keeps delaying. Apple's Safari and Mozilla Firefox already block most third-party cookies by default.

But tracking companies are clever. When one method gets blocked, they invent new ones — fingerprinting (identifying you by your browser settings), first-party data sharing deals, and "privacy-preserving" ad systems that still track you, just with extra steps.

**The honest truth:** these grey practices are still very common in 2025. Laws are chasing them, but the trackers stay one step ahead.

**What can YOU do?**
- Use a browser that blocks trackers (Firefox, Brave, Safari)
- Install an ad blocker (uBlock Origin is free and excellent)
- Check for the cookie popup on websites — actually click "Reject all" instead of "Accept"
- Remember: if a service is free and shows you ads, YOU are the product being sold

The recommendation system in YOUR favorite app is not the villain here. The invisible trackers you never agreed to? Those are the ones to watch out for.
