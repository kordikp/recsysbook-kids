Stop reading for a minute. Seriously. Grab your phone or computer and try one of these. Seeing your own data is worth a thousand explanations.

**YouTube / Google:**
1. Go to [myactivity.google.com](https://myactivity.google.com)
2. Sign in with your Google account
3. Click "YouTube History"
4. Scroll. Keep scrolling. Keep going.

That's every single video you've ever watched on that account. Every search. With dates and times. Some people find YEARS of history here.

**TikTok:**
1. Open TikTok > tap Profile
2. Tap the three lines (menu) > Settings and Privacy
3. Tap Account > Download Your Data
4. Request your data and wait for the file

You'll get a file containing your entire TikTok history -- every video watched, every like, every comment, every search. It can take a few days to arrive.

**Instagram:**
1. Go to Settings > Accounts Center > Your Information and Permissions
2. Tap "Download Your Information"
3. Select your account and request a download

The file includes your messages, searches, ads you interacted with, and a list of interests Instagram thinks you have. That interests list is especially eye-opening.

**Spotify:**
1. Go to [spotify.com/account](https://spotify.com/account)
2. Click Privacy Settings
3. Click "Download Your Data"

You'll see your streaming history, your search queries, and inferences Spotify has made about you.

**Now the good news: you can clean up.**

YouTube lets you auto-delete your history. Go to myactivity.google.com > Activity controls > choose to auto-delete data older than 3 months or 18 months. Do this. It limits how far back the algorithm can "remember."

On TikTok, you can clear your watch history: Profile > Menu > Settings and Privacy > Content Preferences > Clear watch history. This effectively resets your recommendations.

**Think about it:** Did the amount of data surprise you? Most kids -- and most adults -- have no idea how much is stored. Now that you know, you get to decide what to do about it. That's the whole point.
