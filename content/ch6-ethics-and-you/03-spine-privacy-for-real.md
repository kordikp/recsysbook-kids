Adults love to say "be careful with your data online." But they rarely explain what that actually means. So let's be specific.

**What YouTube knows about you:**
Every video you've ever watched. How long you watched each one. What you searched for. What you clicked on and what you skipped. What time of day you're usually active. What device you use. Your approximate location. What ads you've clicked. How fast you scroll.

**What TikTok knows about you:**
Every video you watched. Every video you *skipped* (and how quickly). Videos you rewatched. How long you hovered on a video before scrolling past. What sounds and music you respond to. What visual styles catch your eye. Your typing patterns when you comment. Your face and voice if you've posted videos.

**What Spotify knows about you:**
Every song you've played. How many seconds before you skipped. What you listen to when you're happy vs. sad (yes, it can tell). What time you work out. When you go to sleep. What podcasts you listen to and which parts you rewind.

All this data builds a **model** of you. Think of it as a digital twin -- a mathematical version of you that predicts what you'll do next. It doesn't know your name (usually). It doesn't read your private messages (usually). But it knows your *patterns* with eerie accuracy.

Is this "spying"? That's a loaded word. These companies aren't hiding in your bushes with binoculars. You agreed to their terms of service (even if nobody actually reads those). The data collection happens openly -- it's just that most people don't realize how much data they're generating every second they use an app.

**The real question isn't whether they collect data.** Of course they do -- that's how recommendation systems work. The question is: **do YOU know what they collect? And are YOU OK with it?**

If you didn't know the answer before reading this page, that's a problem. Not because the data collection is necessarily wrong, but because you can't make good decisions about something you don't understand.

Now you understand. So what do you want to do about it?
